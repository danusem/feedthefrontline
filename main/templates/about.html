About

You want to keep the doors open and people employed.
People in your community want to help.
Frontline workers could use a decent meal.

Join now (it's free!) and make all 3 a reality.

What is this, exactly?

It's simple.

  1. Donors select a restaurant in their local community and designate the number of meals they'd like to donate.
  2. Restaurants create and deliver those meals to local hospitals, labs or other frontline facilities.

It's a win-win-win.

  1. Donors show support their local communities, thank the frontline heros.
  2. Restaurants keep the lights on and their people employed.
  3. Frontline heros get a delicious meal and don't have to eat out of a vending machine for once.

Why get involved?

It's free. Why not?!?

You have an opportunity to keep people employed, help those sitting at home feel engaged in their community, AND reach a few new potential customers at the same time.

How do I sign up?

  1. When you sign up, check "I am restaurant".
  2. Fill in the restaurant profile.
    • Put your restaurant name into the Google search field.
    • Your name and location information will automatically populate the form.
    • Put a link to your main website
    • Set the cost per meal donors will pay. This should includes cost of food, wages, sales tax and delivery.
    • Add a short description of your restaurant, perhaps including the number of people you hope to keep employed by participating in this program.
    • Set the weekly goal number of meals you would like to provide.
  3. Contact a hospital or other frontline facility you'd like to donate to.
  4. Once the connection has been established:
    • Add the facility to your profile.
    • Upload your restaurant's logo.
    • Add your PayPal Merchant ID.

And then?

Start advertising your participation!

  1. Check in daily to see how many meals have been donated.
  2. Once you reach your goal number, contact your donation facility and set up a delivery date and time!
  3. Log back in and check off when you've delivered your meals.
